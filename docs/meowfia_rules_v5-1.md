# MEOWFIA — Official Rules (v5)

---

## OVERVIEW

Meowfia is a social deduction game for 4–8 players. Each round, players are secretly assigned as either **Farm** (innocent) or **Meowfia** (cat). Through discussion, deduction, and a little gambling, players try to figure out who the cats are — and bet on their convictions using their cards. Every card in the game is an egg: animals, cats, and flowers all share one deck.

**The twist:** Meowfia players don't know who each other are. And the number of Meowfia changes every round — there could be none, one, several, or even all cats. Nobody knows.

---

## WHAT YOU NEED

- The Meowfia deck (64 cards — see The Deck)
- The Meowfia app on a phone
- Player colour tokens (one per player)

---

## THE DECK

The Meowfia deck contains **64 cards**. Each card has two identities:

1. **The egg** — a suit symbol (♥ ♦ ♣ ♠) and a value (1–13) in the corner, or a **Wild** (no suit, value 0). This is what matters when the card is in your hand.
2. **The animal or flower** — printed in the centre with its name, description, and QR code. This is what matters when the card is in the pool.

The deck is composed of:

- **14 Hearts** ♥ (values 1–13, plus one duplicate)
- **14 Diamonds** ♦ (values 1–13, plus one duplicate)
- **14 Clubs** ♣ (values 1–13, plus one duplicate)
- **14 Spades** ♠ (values 1–13, plus one duplicate)
- **8 Wilds** (value 0, no suit)

Each card also has one of three animal types printed on it:

- **Farm animals** (birds and other farm creatures) — these become Farm roles when placed in the pool
- **Meowfia animals** (cats) — these become Meowfia roles when placed in the pool
- **Flowers** — these trigger a special event for the round when revealed into the pool

When a card is in the pool, its animal or flower description matters. When a card is in your hand, only the **suit** and **value** matter for throwing, keeping, and scoring.

### The Four Suits

Each suit has its own personality. When you look at your hand before a vote, the suit on each card tells you what kind of bet you're making.

| Suit | Symbol | Identity | When You Win | When You Lose |
|------|--------|----------|-------------|---------------|
| **Hearts** | ♥ | The Safe Bet | Bank the card | Nothing — card is simply discarded |
| **Diamonds** | ♦ | Riches & Rags | Bank the card + lock in 1 card from your hand to your score pile | Card is discarded + move 1 card from your score pile back to your hand |
| **Clubs** | ♣ | Terrifying Power | Bank the card (always counts as **2 votes**) | Card goes to your **target's** score pile |
| **Spades** | ♠ | Political & Social | Bank the card + steal 1 card from your **target's** hand | Card is discarded + give 1 card from your hand to your **target** |

**Wilds** (value 0) behave like Hearts when thrown — they bank on a win and have no penalty on a loss. They are worth 0 points in your score pile but still count as 1 vote.

---

## SCORING AT A GLANCE

Your **score pile** is a personal face-down pile of cards you've banked from winning throws across the game. Your score at the end of the game is:

**Final Score = total value of cards in your score pile + 1 point per card remaining in your hand**

Cards in your score pile are worth their printed value (1–13). Cards in your hand are each worth 1 point regardless of value.

---

## KEY TERMS

Several roles and effects reference the following terms. Learn these before playing.

**Wink:** Some roles grant a player a Wink. During the day phase, a player holding a Wink may secretly wink at another player. That player must cease talking and **die** 5–10 seconds later. A Wink is a one-use ability — once used, it is spent. The wink must be subtle; if others notice, that's part of the game.

**Hug (Roleblock):** When a role says it "hugs" a player, that player is **roleblocked**. Their visit still goes through (they still visit their chosen target), but all of their role's passive and active effects are cancelled. For example, a hugged Pigeon will visit their target but will not lay an egg; a hugged Owl will visit but will not learn any animals. A hugged Meowfia player will not steal — the steal is part of the role's action and is blocked like any other effect.

**Confusion:** A confused player receives **incorrect information** in their dawn report (or from night abilities like the Owl). This can include a wrong egg count, wrong animal names, or other misleading details. A confused player must still act on the information they are given — for example, placing eggs in their nest based on their (incorrect) reported count.

**Death:** When a player dies (from a Wink, Stinging Bush, Tickler, Tigerlily, or any other effect), they must immediately **collapse and act out a dramatic death**. Dead players cannot talk for the remainder of the day phase. Dead players **can still throw eggs** during eggsecution, but their thrown cards **do not count as votes**. Dead players' thrown cards are still resolved normally for scoring — they bank cards on a win, suffer suit penalties on a loss, and receive the correct-target consolation like anyone else. Death during the day does not remove a player from consideration for eggsecution — eggs already thrown at a dead player still count.

**Nest Maximum:** A player's nest can hold a maximum of **5 eggs**. Any eggs beyond 5 fall out and are not counted. This applies to all players, including the Persian.

## GAME SETUP

1. Players sit in a circle. The person who most recently touched a cat is the first dealer.
2. The dealer finds the "Pigeon" and "House Cat" cards from the deck and places them face-up in the centre to form the **pool**. Players can read each card's animal description to learn what that role does.
3. The dealer shuffles the remaining deck.
4. Each player is dealt **3 cards** from the deck to form their starting hand. Keep your hand hidden from other players.

---

## PLAYING A ROUND

Each round follows these phases in order:

### 1. Pool Phase

The dealer reveals the top 3 cards from the deck and adds them face-up to the pool. These cards are now active for the round — their descriptions are visible to all players.

- **Animal cards** become available roles for this round. Players can read what each role does.
- **Flower cards** trigger their special event for this round (or as described on the card).

It's possible to reveal any mix — three animals, two animals and a flower, one animal and two flowers, or even three flowers. Whatever comes off the top of the deck is what you get.

These cards are removed from the deck and will not appear in anyone's hand.

The dealer opens the Meowfia app, presses "Start Game," and scans the QR codes on all cards currently in the pool.

### 2. Setup Phase

Each player **draws 2 cards** from the deck and adds them to their hand.

Players pass the phone clockwise. Each player enters their name and is **secretly** assigned their role by the app. Each player has an independent **1 in 3 chance** of being Meowfia. This means:

- There might be zero Meowfia this round
- There might be one, two, three, or more
- In rare cases, everyone could be Meowfia
- **Meowfia players do not know who else is Meowfia**

The app assigns each player a role from the pool. **Pigeon** and **House Cat** act as buffer roles — if all other Farm or Meowfia roles in the pool are already assigned, additional players of that alignment receive Pigeon (Farm) or House Cat (Meowfia). **Multiple players can have the same role**, including non-buffer roles if the pool is small relative to the player count.

### 3. Night Phase

**All players close their eyes.** Players pass the phone in a circle. When the phone reaches you, open your eyes, read your night action, and close them again before passing the phone. The app tells each player their role's night action — for example, visiting another player, laying eggs, stealing eggs, or learning information.

**Meowfia players steal:** every Meowfia role's action includes stealing an egg from the player they visit. The stolen egg goes into the Meowfia player's nest, giving them cover to claim a Farm role. However, the steal is part of the role's action — if a Meowfia player is roleblocked (hugged), they will not steal.

During the night phase, players do not physically place eggs yet. The app tracks everything. Each player can only visit one other player per night.

This phase should take about 1 minute.

### 4. Dawn Phase

**Players keep their eyes closed.** Players pass the phone one more time. When the phone reaches you, open your eyes to read your dawn report, then close them again before passing it on. Each player is told:

- How many eggs are in their nest
- Any other important information (e.g. if their role changed, if they were confused)

After everyone has seen their dawn report, all players open their eyes and immediately:

1. **Draw cards** from the deck equal to the number of eggs in your nest (as reported by the app). These cards join your hand. If the app reports more eggs than the deck can supply, draw as many as possible. Nests hold a maximum of 5 eggs.
2. **Place cards** face-down in front of you equal to the number of eggs the app reported. Choose which cards from your hand to place — these form your visible "nest." Other players can see how many cards are in your nest, but not their suit or value.

### 5. Day Phase (5 minutes)

The dealer places the phone face-up in the centre and taps "Next" to start the timer.

Players now have **5 minutes** to discuss:

- Who they think is Meowfia
- What they learned during the night
- What the eggs in each player's nest might mean
- Who they plan to target in the vote
- Any flower event card effects that can be activated during the day

**Early Vote:** At any point, a player may yell **"CAW CAW"** to call for an early vote. If at least **three players** have called "CAW CAW" (at any point during the day, not necessarily at the same time), the day ends immediately and voting begins.

### 6. Voting Phase (Eggsecution)

**No talking during voting.** Any player who speaks loses one card from their hand (discard it).

Players pick up any cards from their nest back into their hand.

**Dead players** may still throw eggs, but their thrown cards **do not count as votes**. Their cards are still resolved normally for scoring — dead players can still win points.

Each player (living or dead) now secretly chooses:

1. **A target** — place a player colour token in your closed fist, choosing any other player to throw your eggs at
2. **Cards to throw** — select any number of cards from your hand (minimum 1) and hold them with the token. Choose carefully — the suit of each card determines what happens if you win or lose.

Players **keep** any cards they chose not to throw. These stay in hand and are worth **1 point each** at the end of the game.

Once everyone is ready, all players **reveal simultaneously** — showing their target token and thrown cards face-up.

**Counting votes:** each thrown card from a **living** player counts as 1 vote, **except Clubs ♣ which count as 2 votes each**. Dead players' thrown cards are not counted as votes. The player who receives the most votes thrown at them is **eggsecuted** (eliminated for that round). Players throw eggs at each other — you cannot throw at yourself.

- **Ties go to Meowfia** — if two or more players are tied for most votes, the tied player who is Farm is eliminated. If all tied players are the same team, one is chosen at random.

## WINNING, LOSING, AND RESOLVING CARDS

After the eliminated player's alignment is revealed:

- If the eliminated player is **Meowfia** → all **Farm** players win
- If the eliminated player is **Farm** → all **Meowfia** players win

### Resolving Thrown Cards

Starting with the player to the left of the dealer and going clockwise, each player resolves their thrown cards one at a time:

**If your team WON this round:**

Each card you threw goes into your **score pile** (face-down, personal pile — these accumulate across the entire game). Then apply the card's suit bonus:

| Suit | Win Bonus |
|------|-----------|
| ♥ Hearts | No bonus — card is simply banked |
| ♦ Diamonds | Lock in 1 card from your hand: move your highest-value hand card into your score pile |
| ♣ Clubs | No bonus (the 2-vote power was already applied during elimination) |
| ♠ Spades | Steal 1 card at random from your **target's** hand into your hand |

**If your team LOST this round:**

First, check: **did you target a player on the opposite team?** If yes, you get a consolation — pick the single highest-value card from among your thrown cards and bank it into your score pile. This card does not trigger any suit penalty. (For example, if you're Farm, you lost, but you threw at a Meowfia player — you were right about *them* even though your team lost. You get to keep your best thrown card.)

All remaining thrown cards (or all thrown cards, if you targeted your own team) go into the **shared discard pile**. Then apply each discarded card's suit penalty:

| Suit | Loss Penalty |
|------|-------------|
| ♥ Hearts | No penalty |
| ♦ Diamonds | Move 1 card from your score pile back into your hand (highest value first). If your score pile is empty, this penalty is skipped. |
| ♣ Clubs | This card goes into your **target's** score pile instead of the discard pile |
| ♠ Spades | Give 1 card from your hand to your **target** (they add it to their hand) |

If you cannot pay a penalty (e.g. no cards in hand for ♠, no cards in score pile for ♦), that penalty is skipped.

**Special cases:**

- **No Meowfia this round:** A Farm player will inevitably be eliminated. Meowfia "wins" — meaning everyone's thrown cards are discarded with their loss penalties applied. The witch hunt was pointless.
- **All Meowfia this round:** A Meowfia player will inevitably be eliminated, so Farm "wins" — even though there is no Farm. One Meowfia gets sacrificed while the rest profit.

## QUICK REFERENCE

| What Happened | Result |
|--------------|--------|
| Threw ♥, team **won** | Card → score pile |
| Threw ♥, team **lost** | Card → discard (no penalty) |
| Threw ♦, team **won** | Card → score pile + move best hand card → score pile |
| Threw ♦, team **lost** | Card → discard + move best score pile card → hand |
| Threw ♣, team **won** | Card → score pile (counted as 2 votes) |
| Threw ♣, team **lost** | Card → **target's** score pile |
| Threw ♠, team **won** | Card → score pile + steal 1 from **target's** hand |
| Threw ♠, team **lost** | Card → discard + give 1 from hand to **target** |
| Threw Wild, team **won** | Card → score pile (worth 0) |
| Threw Wild, team **lost** | Card → discard (no penalty) |
| Kept any card | Worth 1 point at end of game |
| Lost, but targeted opposite team | Bank your best thrown card (no suit effects) |
| Dead player throws | Resolved normally for scoring, but cards don't count as votes |

## POST-ROUND

1. Verify all thrown cards have been resolved (banked or discarded with suit effects applied).
2. The dealer passes to the next player clockwise.
3. The new dealer reveals the top 3 cards from the deck and adds them face-up to the pool (any animals expand the available roles; any flowers trigger their event for the new round).
4. Start a new round.

If the draw pile runs out, shuffle the discard pile to form a new draw pile. If both the draw pile and discard pile are empty, the game ends immediately.

After **3 rounds** (or however many were agreed upon), the game ends. Each player calculates their final score:

**Final Score = total value of all cards in your score pile + 1 point per card remaining in your hand**

The player with the **highest score** wins the game.

## SUIT STRATEGY

**Hearts ♥** are the safest throw. No penalty for losing means you can throw them with low confidence. Hearts are ideal for hedging — throw them at a suspect you're only mildly sure about. The downside: no win bonus either. Hearts are pure, clean value.

**Diamonds ♦** are boom or bust. Winning with a Diamond banks the card *and* locks in your best hand card — potentially two high-value cards scored from a single throw. But losing with a Diamond pulls a card out of your score pile and demotes it back to your hand, where it's only worth 1 point. A ♦K won is a 13-point swing in your favour; a ♦K lost while holding a banked King could cost you 12 points. Throw Diamonds when you're sure. Keep them when you're not — a Diamond in hand is still worth 1 point with zero risk.

**Clubs ♣** are political weapons. Each Club counts as **2 votes** toward elimination, making them twice as powerful for determining who gets eggsecuted. But losing with a Club is devastating: the Club card itself goes into your *target's* score pile, giving them its face value as free points. A ♣K thrown at the wrong person hands them 13 points. Clubs should be thrown when you're confident about your target AND you need the extra vote weight to secure the elimination. A low Club (♣2, ♣3) is much safer to throw wrong than a high one.

**Spades ♠** are targeted and personal. Winning steals a card from your target's hand, weakening them and growing your options for future rounds. Losing gives one of your cards to your target, strengthening the very person you accused. Everyone at the table sees who you Spaded — it's a public accusation. Spades create direct player-to-player interaction and catch-up dynamics: steal from whoever is winning, and losing a Spade hurts less when you're already behind.

**Wilds** are worth 0 in your score pile but cost nothing to lose. Throw them freely as extra votes with zero risk.

**Keeping cards** is always worth 1 point per card, regardless of suit or value. A King of Spades (value 13) kept in hand is worth only 1 point — the same as a 2 of Hearts. But banked through a winning throw, that King is worth 13. The gap between keeping (1 point) and correctly throwing (card value) is what makes the gambling meaningful.

**The correct-target consolation** rewards reading the room even when your team loses. If you threw at an enemy player and your team still lost, you get to keep your best thrown card. A conservative player who throws a single high Heart at the right person banks it regardless of the team result. This means being *right about who the enemy is* always has value — even when the vote doesn't go your way.

## TIPS FOR NEW PLAYERS

**Hearts first.** When in doubt, throw your Hearts. Zero penalty means zero regret.

**Watch the suits people throw.** After eggsecution, all thrown cards are revealed face-up. A player who threw Clubs was very confident — they risked giving their target free points. A player who threw all Hearts was hedging. Track these patterns across rounds.

**Low Diamonds, high confidence.** Diamonds are safest when their face value is low. A ♦2 won is still a great deal (bank it + lock in another card), and a ♦2 lost barely hurts. Save your high Diamonds for when you're sure.

**Clubs are for close votes.** If you think the vote will be tight, a single Club swings 2 votes. That can be the difference between eggsecuting the right player and losing to a tie (which favours Meowfia). But never throw a high Club casually — a ♣K in the wrong hands is a gift.

**Spades are accusations.** When you throw a Spade at someone, everyone at the table notices. Win or lose, it's a statement. Use Spades to weaken a player you're confident about, or to send a message during the next day phase.

**Keep high-value cards when unsure.** A 13 in your hand is worth 1 point kept but 13 banked through a win. Only throw high cards when confident. Throw low cards more freely.

**Watch the nest eggs.** The cards placed in front of players during dawn carry clues about what happened at night. Cross-reference nest sizes with claimed roles to catch liars.

**Remember: Meowfia doesn't know each other.** Meowfia can accidentally vote for their own teammates. Don't assume coordinated behaviour means someone is innocent.

**Meowfia steals.** If your egg count is lower than expected at dawn, a cat visited you. If someone has more eggs than expected, they might have stolen. Track the missing eggs.

**Zero-Meowfia rounds can happen.** If night phase information seems unusually quiet, consider that there might be no cats at all. Every egg you throw is a loss in that scenario.

**Your hand is your safety net.** Every card in your hand at the end of the game is worth 1 point. Don't throw everything unless you're sure.

**Target the right team even if you lose.** The correct-target consolation lets you bank your best thrown card even when your team loses — as long as you threw at an enemy. Being right about *who* always pays off.

## FIRST GAME MODE (Recommended)

For your first game, keep it simple:

**Round 1:** The pool contains only **Pigeon** and **House Cat** (no extra cards revealed). Every Farm player is a Pigeon, every Meowfia player is a House Cat. Focus on learning the flow: night → dawn → day → vote → score. Don't worry about complex roles.

**Round 2:** Reveal 1-2 cards into the pool. Read what each new role does. Now a few players will have different night actions, giving more information to discuss during the day.

**Round 3+:** Reveal the full 3 cards per round. By now everyone understands the voting and scoring — the new roles just add more clues to work with.

This lets new players learn the core gambling loop (which suits to throw, when to keep, how to read the vote) before adding the deduction layer on top.
