# MEOWFIA — Flower Event Cards Reference

Every flower card is shuffled into the same deck as animal cards. When a flower is revealed during the Pool Phase, its event activates for that round. Like all cards, flowers have an egg in the corner — if a flower ends up in your hand instead of the pool, it's just an egg. Some flowers are discarded after use; others affect the whole round.

---

## Information Flowers

These flowers modify what players know or learn.

**Sunflower** — At any point during the day, a player can scan this card and spend one egg from their hand to secretly learn if a chosen player is a certain animal or not. Discard this card afterwards.

**Bookworm** — Farm players will be given an identical secret word at night, while Meowfia players will be given a different identical word. At the end of the day, the eggsecuted player may guess the word of both teams from a list of options. If they guess a team's word correctly, that team wins instead.

**Pitcher Plant** — After the night phase, the app announces who visited whom — but not their roles or what happened during the visit. The full visit graph is public knowledge before the day phase begins.

**Cactus Flower** — Before the day phase, the app reveals exactly how many Meowfia players there are this round (but not who they are). If there are zero Meowfia, this is announced and the round is immediately skipped — no discussion, no vote, no eggs lost. Everyone scores +1 per egg in hand.

**Lotus** — During the Pool Phase, the 3 new cards are added to the pool face-down instead of face-up. Players do not know what roles or flowers were added this round. The app scans them normally and assigns roles from the full pool (including the hidden cards). At the end of the round, after scoring, the hidden cards are revealed.

**Corpse Flower** — This round, the eggsecuted player's role and alignment are not revealed. Players do not learn whether Farm or Meowfia won. Scoring for this round is hidden and only added to the scoresheet at the end of the game.

---

## Communication Flowers

These flowers change how players communicate during the day.

**Banksia** — Players cannot talk normally this round. Communication is limited to whispering in the ear of the player sitting next to you. Players may freely make bird sounds or meow.

**Flannel Flower** — Players must keep their eyes shut during the entire day phase. Discussion continues normally — you just can't see anyone.

**Marigold** — This round, the normal day phase discussion is replaced by structured speeches. Starting from the dealer and going clockwise, each player has exactly 30 seconds to plead their case — share their night information, make accusations, defend themselves, or say whatever they want. No other player may speak during someone's 30 seconds. After all players have spoken, there is a final 60-second open discussion before voting begins. Discard this card afterwards.

**Tumbleweed** — At the start of the day phase, all players must stand up from their seats. There are no assigned seats for this round's discussion — players walk freely around the room while discussing. Players may form clusters, break away, whisper to someone across the room, or stand alone. When the day timer expires, players return to the table for eggsecution as normal.

---

## Day Phase Flowers

These flowers modify the structure or length of the day phase.

**Bluebell** — At any point during the day, a single player may audibly say "CAW CAW" to gain one egg to their hand and immediately end the day (overriding the normal three-player requirement). Players must stop talking immediately. Discard this card afterwards.

**Nightshade** — For the next round, there will be a Dusk phase before the Night phase. During Dusk, players can talk openly and discuss their plans for the night. Discard this card afterwards.

**Mulberry** — This round, the day phase does not have a fixed 5-minute timer. Instead, the timer starts at 30 seconds. When it expires, any player may spend one egg from their hand to buy another 30 seconds. If no one pays within 5 seconds of the timer expiring, the day ends immediately and voting begins. There is no cap on how many extensions can be bought.

**Stinging Bush** — During the day, the first player to physically touch another player assassinates them. That player immediately dies and cannot participate in the vote. Discard this card afterwards.

---

## Night Phase Flowers

These flowers modify the night phase or role assignment.

**Moonflower** — This round has two consecutive Night phases before the Dawn phase. Players perform their night action twice (they may choose different targets each night). Dawn reports combine the results of both nights, and nest eggs stack. The nest egg cap of 5 is removed for this round.

**Wolfsbane** — This round, the chance of being assigned Meowfia is 1 in 2 instead of 1 in 3. The app will announce that Wolfsbane is active before roles are assigned.

**Twinflower** — This round, there is no limit on how many players can share the same role. Any role in the pool may be assigned to any number of players.

**Angel's Trumpet** — This round, egg laying and stealing between players are inverted. Whenever a role would lay an egg in another player's nest, it steals one from them instead. Whenever a role would steal an egg from another player, it lays one in their nest instead. Self-directed egg actions (laying in your own nest) and egg transfers between other players are unaffected. All other role effects (investigation, confusion, hugging, etc.) work normally.

**Dandelion** — This round, night actions resolve in seating order (clockwise from the dealer) instead of the app's normal resolution order. The player sitting closest to the dealer acts first, and the player furthest around the circle acts last. Earlier actions take effect before later ones — a Floofer who sits before an Owl will hug the Owl before the Owl can investigate. The app will announce that Dandelion is active before the night phase.

**Wildflower** — A strange event will happen during the night. The app will randomly choose one: a random player receives an extra egg, two players swap roles, a player gets a Wink, eggs are doubled, or something else unexpected. Discard this card afterwards.

---

## Nest & Egg Flowers

These flowers modify nests, eggs, or hand management.

**Chocolate Lily** — Egg counts in nests will be hidden this round. Instead of placing eggs in their nests, players draw the eggs into their hand and hide their hand size from other players. Players may lie about their hand size.

**Foxglove** — During the day phase, players may openly trade egg cards from their hands with each other. Both players must agree to a trade, and all trades are public — both cards are briefly revealed face-up to the table before being exchanged. A player may make as many trades as they wish. Discard this card afterwards.

---

## Voting & Eggsecution Flowers

These flowers change how the vote or elimination works.

**Bottlebrush** — During the eggsecution, players may throw eggs at this flower card (placed in the centre alongside the player targets). If this flower receives the most eggs, nobody is eliminated — the game continues for another night and day cycle. All eggs thrown at the flower are lost. Discard this card afterwards.

**Tigerlily** — During the day, players may vote prematurely by placing eggs from their hand in front of the player they suspect. Players may split their eggs between multiple targets. Players will randomly die at 30-second intervals throughout the day, with all players being dead by the end (unable to throw more eggs). The player with the most eggs in front of them is eggsecuted.

**Snapdragon** — This round, the eggsecution is replaced by an open auction. Instead of simultaneous voting, players take turns clockwise starting from the dealer. On your turn, you may place one or more eggs from your hand face-down in front of any player. You may also pass. The auction continues around the table until every player passes consecutively without anyone placing eggs. The player with the most eggs in front of them is eggsecuted. No talking during the auction.

**Poppy** — During eggsecution, players must shuffle their hand face-down before selecting eggs to throw. You choose how many eggs to throw, but draw them randomly from your shuffled hand without looking. You do not learn which egg colors you threw until scoring.

**Venus Flytrap** — This round, the two players who receive the most eggs thrown at them are both eggsecuted. Each elimination is scored independently — if your target was eliminated and your team won that elimination, your thrown eggs score as wins. If your target was eliminated and your team lost that elimination, your thrown eggs score as losses. If your target was not eliminated, your thrown eggs score nothing.

---

## Win Condition & Scoring Flowers

These flowers change how winning, losing, or scoring works.

**Wax Flower** — During eggsecution, you win if you threw eggs at any player on the opposite team, regardless of who was actually eggsecuted. Likewise, you lose if you threw eggs only at players on your own team. This replaces the normal win/loss condition for this round.

**Crimson Kunzea** — Before you have spoken during the day, you may hit the table with your fist to double down. Your final score for this round is doubled — whether positive or negative. Once you have spoken a single word, the opportunity is gone. Discard this card afterwards.

**Oleander** — This round, kept eggs are worth 0 points instead of 1. The only way to score positive points is to throw eggs on the winning side.

**Thistle** — At the start of this round, the app displays the current scoreboard and each player's running total is announced publicly. The player currently in last place becomes the Grudge Player. During eggsecution, the Grudge Player's thrown eggs count double — each egg counts as 2 eggs toward elimination AND scores double points if their team wins. If multiple players are tied for last, all tied players become Grudge Players.

---

## Social & Physical Flowers

These flowers add physical or social mechanics.

**Golden Wattle** — At any point during the day, two players may shake hands (if they haven't before) to signal a bond. At the end of the round, if bonded players were the same alignment, they both get +3 points. If they were different alignments, the Farm player gets −3 points while the Meowfia player gets +3 points.

**Desert Pea** — During the day, a player may stand up and declare a challenge. That player bets any amount of eggs from their hand, scans this card, and proceeds to guess each player's animal in the app. If correct, the app announces their victory — they are the sole winner of the round. If incorrect, the app announces they have been killed and they lose their bet. Discard this card afterwards.

---

## Round Structure Flowers

These flowers modify the overall round structure.

**Waratah** — For the next round, players draft an animal from their hand to be their role. Since every card in your hand has an animal or flower printed on it, you choose one animal card to be your role — that card is removed from your hand, leaving you with 4 eggs for voting. (You cannot draft a flower card as your role.) The app still assigns alignment independently at 1 in 3 chance.

**Amaranth** — This round, after the first eggsecution and scoring, the round does not end. One additional cycle of Night → Dawn → Day → Eggsecution is played with the same pool and remaining players. The eliminated player sits out for the second cycle. Scoring applies normally to both cycles.

**Bird of Paradise** — This flower adds an extra "bot" player to the game. The bot visits a random player at night and can be Meowfia or Farm. The bot does not throw eggs during voting but may be eggsecuted. If the bot is eliminated, scoring resolves based on the bot's alignment as normal.
